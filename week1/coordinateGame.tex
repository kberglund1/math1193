\title{Coordinate Game}
\author{Kenneth Berglund}

\begin{document}
\begin{abstract}
    Playing a game involving graphing equations on the coordinate plane.
\end{abstract}
\maketitle

Let's play a game!

\begin{itemize}
    \item Consider the equation \(y = 2 + 3x\). Pair up and 
    choose one person to be Player X and the other
    to be Player Y.
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Player X comes up with 3 \(x\)-values, and 
        Player Y comes up with 3 \(y\)-values. 
        These can be any numbers you want, but try 
        to keep them small so they can be plotted 
        later.
        \item The players exchange their numbers. 
        Player X has to find the \(x\)-values that 
        correspond to the \(y\)-values and put 
        them into coordinate pairs, and Player Y 
        has to find the \(y\)-values that 
        correspond to the \(x\)-values and put 
        them into coordinate pairs. 
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Whose job is easier?
        \end{itemize}
        \item Write down the 6 
        ordered pairs that satisfy the equation.
        \item Graph your ordered pairs on the \(xy\)
        coordinate plane.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{itemize}
